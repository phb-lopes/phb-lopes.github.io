About me

I am a PhD student in Bioinformatics at UFRN. I have a master's degree in Bioinformatics from the Federal University of Rio Grande do Norte - UFRN (2021) and graduation in Computer Science from the State University of Rio Grande do Norte - UERN (2019). In my graduation, I used computer vision algorithms with a Convolutional Neural Network to develop a tool capable of tracking and recognizing the different behaviors presented by octopus paralarvae (Octopus insularis). In my master's, I utilized an agent-based model to assess the impact of non-pharmaceutical governmental interventions in Natal-RN (Brazil) city during the Covid-19 pandemic. Simultaneously, I contributed to the investigation of sleep in adult octopuses (Octopus insularis), mostly in data analysis and visualization of the results. I also examined the replicability problems and the lack of computational numerical precision by exploring the activity of a Hodgkin-Huxley-type neural network model. Currently, in my PhD, I am analyzing rat’s brain electrophysiological signal and as an exchange period at the Max Planck Institute in Germany; I am developing a pipeline able to recognize and track marmosets (Callithrix jacchus) during a foraging behavior.
